    01 · Founder problem

    A two-person WordPress consultancy gets a 90-day ISO 27001 demand.

    The customer I keep coming back to

    Two people. Ten client sites. One Droplet. Not a security team. Not an enterprise buyer. They chose DigitalOcean because it was simple, affordable, fast.

    Then their biggest client updates the renewal contract:

    “We need ISO 27001 readiness in ninety days, or we can’t sign.”

    They didn’t buy DO for compliance. But now compliance is what determines whether they stay on DO.

    Three bad options today

    1. Hire a compliance consultant they can’t afford.
    2. Interpret ISO language themselves and lose weeks.
    3. Migrate to a platform that looks more enterprise-ready — even though they didn’t want the complexity.

    The product isn’t certification. It’s the bridge from panic to a credible client response.

    Speaker note · why this customer

    This is mass-market DigitalOcean — real budget pressure, real client demand, no security staff. If DO doesn’t help them through that moment, someone else will, and they migrate. The retention math is the product.

    02 · Why DigitalOcean should care

    This is a retention moment masquerading as a compliance task.

    Retention trigger Client contract renewal blocked on framework readiness
    Consultant-led ISO 27001 cost — SMB £10k–£20k small/medium business range, per Hightable
    Full consultant-led support $30k–$50k per ISMS.online

    The framing

    The plugin doesn’t promise certification. It helps the customer deflect the first expensive consultant call and reduce the need for immediate consultant-led triage — long enough to give a credible client response and keep building on DO.

    Not “you’re ISO compliant.” Just: here is your readiness posture against the evidence your client may ask for.

    What DO actually gets

    • Retention of accounts at the contract-pressure moment
    • Trust deepened at the moment DO becomes business-critical
    • Loyalty loop — small shops tell other small shops who helped them
    • Brand fit — practical, clear, built for teams without dedicated security staff

    Cost figures are reference points for the consultant-led path the plugin helps customers delay or deflect — not a guaranteed saving.

    03 · Complementary, not competitive

    DO CSPM tells customers what’s misconfigured today. This plugin tells them whether their architecture meets the controls auditors will check tomorrow.

    Dimension DO CSPM security-compliance plugin
    LayerRuntime postureDesign-time architecture compliance
    InputLive DO resourcesArchitecture description

    CadenceContinuous scan~90-second point-in-time assessment

    The one-liner

    “CSPM tells you what’s misconfigured today on DO. My plugin tells you whether your architecture even meets the controls auditors will check tomorrow. Same trust narrative, different layers.”

    Why it sits above CSPM, not against it

    DO’s CSPM launch announcement shows the right DNA for runtime — agentless, AI-assisted, in-dashboard. See also the CSPM product page and CSPM docs. The framework-compliance layer sits naturally above that, not against it.

    04 · Solution surface

    One plugin. Four slash commands, a guidance skill, a security-auditor subagent.

    slash command

    /compliance-check

    Phased applicability → control map → gap analysis → roadmap → evidence ledger → leadership-ready summary.

    slash command

    /spot-check

    Targeted scan for one control or framework concern — 90 seconds, no project setup.

    slash command

    /edit-docx

    Applies tracked-change edits to a Word document — useful for policy templates and client responses.

    slash command

    /compliance-eval

    Runs the evaluation harness against a configured suite. Catches regression in framework reasoning.

    skill

    compliance-guidance

    Auto-loaded when the model reasons about compliance scope. Keeps the promise narrow.

    subagent

    security-auditor

    Identity-architecture / compliance auditor: IAM, CIAM, RBAC, workload identity.

    Turns an architecture description into applicability, control mapping, gap analysis, roadmap, evidence ledger, and a leadership-ready summary — without certifying anything.

    06 · Demo

    Spot-check, in the wild.

    Sample commands from the install guide

    /security-compliance:compliance-check Stripe-style payments API serving EU + US merchants. --jurisdictions EU,US --industry fintech --strict-facts
    /security-compliance:spot-check Are 5-minute OIDC workload tokens enough to satisfy DORA Art. 9(4)(c) strong authentication for ICT services?

    07 · Validation & metrics

    Measure behavior change, not reports generated.

    North star Retention of accounts at a compliance-triggered moment

    Did the customer stay on DO when a client framework demand showed up, or did they migrate?

    Leading · activation Plugin installed → first scan completed → client-facing response generated

    Funnel from install to a deliverable the customer can actually send.

    Leading · behavior change MFA on · access tightened · backups configured · stale users removed · access-review documented

    Account-level changes after a scan — the real signal the customer acted on the gap analysis.

    Vanity output Reports generated

    Looks good in a deck. Doesn’t prove a customer stayed, paid, or changed anything. Tracked, not optimized for.

    Engineered cuts, not residual: full compliance management is out of scope by design. The product owns one moment — the contract-pressure trigger.
